Bontoc, Mountain Province

Bontoc has been, for years, lurking behind the shadows of its renowned neighbors – Sagada and Banaue. Today, though, Bontoc is amazingly flourishing, and is making some buzz on the tourist radar. For those who are itching for a not-so typical outdoor adventure, this modest mountainous destination will put you on a state of pure bliss with its towering peaks and awe-inspiring sights, like the Maligcong Rice Terraces, Mount Kupapey and Mount Fato.

Siquijor

Want to escape the hustle bustle city life? Yearning for a memorable beach escape? Then, pack your bags, and explore the mystical island of Siquijor! Even though it’s slowly becoming a beloved travel destination, much of the island province is undeveloped. As a matter of fact, Siquijor has barely any public transportation or streetlight at night. What’s more, it is loaded with natural and historic attractions alike, such as Cambugahay Falls, Lazi Church, Capilay Spring Park, Paliton Beach, Kagusuan Beach, Guiwanon Spring Park, Salagdoong Beach, and Century-Old Enchanted Balete Tree.

Kaparkan Falls, Abra

Looks a little familiar? At first glance, this stunning cascade looks like a famous terraced waterfall in Vietnam or Laos. But, guess what? You can lay eyes on this spectacular nature-engineered spring terraces with a visit to Caganayan, Tineg, Abra. And, while getting there isn’t a walk in the park, you’ll be rewarded with a pleasant and refreshing view that will add more life to your Instagram feed. Of course, it also has refreshingly cold waters that will help alleviate stress as well as relax your jaded muscles.

Bukidnon

Bukidnon is, without a shadow of a doubt, a picturesque wonderland that should be in every Pinoy adventurer’s bucket list. Billed as the ultimate destination for mountaineers and intrepid travelers, this province is, after all, home to 4 of the 10 highest peaks in the Philippines – Mount Dulang-Dulang, Mount Kitanglad, Mount Maagnaw and Mount Kalatungan. Even when you’re driving on the highway, you can witness grand views of the province’s canyons, mountains and hills. Moreover, Bukidnon is home to an impressive collection of unexplored natural wonders, including caves, waterfalls, rivers and lakes.

Guimaras

Think Guimaras is all about its world-class mangoes? The truth is, there’s more to this island province than its incredibly delicious and world famous fruit. As long as you’re up for anything, you can capture fully the beauty of Guimaras in an array of ways, including sightseeing, island hopping, knowledge-based tours, local culture, history and adventure. And in spite of its burgeoning tourism, Guimaras isn’t very developed, and is jam-packed with lovely fauna and flora.

Onuk Island, Balabac, Palawan

No list of the best and must-see destinations in the Philippine is complete without the inclusion of Palawan’s most splendid hidden gem – Onuk Island. Sure, it will take great a great deal of time and effort on your end to get there, but you’ll at least have an epic getaway that you’ll remember for years to come. Offering a taste of paradise of earth, this extremely remote island will delight your wanderlust with its enthralling sandbars, red corals, and glowing white sands. Not to mention, the island is visited frequently by many different sea creatures, like whale sharks, sea turtles and brainy dolphins.

Mati City, Davao Oriental

Mati was, once, one of Davao Oriental’s best kept secrets, until it was catapulted into the limelight by a skimboarding competition. Known as one of the rising stars of Mindanao’s tourism scene, Mati brims with surprises for those who are willing to explore this city. Clean, beautiful and free from the seas of tourists, the city has quite a few awesome diversions to offer to the fit traveler as well, like surfing, skimboarding and even ultra-light plane rides.

Carnaza Island, Cebu

Traveling to Cebu? Trust us, there’s no better place in Cebu to go on a soul-searching retreat than the island of Carnaza. With its fresh sea breeze, remote location and laid-back island charm, Carnaza will definitely give you a relaxing break like no other. Furthermore, the island has a plethora of sights that will unleash our inner shutterbug, including unique rock formations, crystal clear waters and pristine white sands. To make things even better, it’s located near to a couple of other remarkable spots – Malapascua Island and Masbate’s La Manok Island.

Tablas Island, Romblon

Home to a number of secluded beaches and a vibrant marine sanctuary teeming with life, Tablas Island is a fantastic destination for those who love the sea. If you’re a diver, make sure you check out the massive Blue Hole—it looks like it came straight out of a sci-fi movie.

Famed for being the Marble Capital of the Philippines, the province's marble is comparable to the world's best. Trade plays a big role in Romblon's economy by virtue of its location at the center of the archipelago.The islands are blessed with natural beauty such as beaches, rivers, mountains, and diverse eco-systems, which offer tourists many opportunities for adventures like scuba, trekking, and snorkeling.
